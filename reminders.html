Paperless Post: iOS Reminder

About the Product

Paperless Post is an e-commerce product company offering beautifully customizable invitations + online event management tools. Compared to desktop, the iOS app offers a more basic feature offering for customization + event tools to allow for a quicker mobile experience.


Role

The "iOS" team manages all features of the platform. It consisted of a small team of a PM, lead PD (me), lead dev, 2 devs, and QA.

Scope

Defined MVP strategy and design for the Reminder feature, which aimed to increase habitual sending from the homepage.

Time

I worked on this project from December 2016-Feb 2017.

Reminder Feature

This feature reminds users of personal upcoming events (holidays, birthdays, anniversaries, etc.) for which they can send a card to their loved ones. Upon setting up reminders, users are essentially setting up their own personalized homepage of sending opportunities related to important events in their lives. The plan was to compare conversion between users sending a card through this feature vs. through generic homepage categories.


Initial Ideation

In ideation, we started by casting a wide net for where we saw this feature headed:

  • Content: What types of reminders can we design for?
  • Value: How can reminders be incentivized and made actionable?
  • Integration: How might we integrate with iOS + rest of app?


    • MVP Scope

    We refined all ideas per category to those shown on the map (see left). We then prioritized the ideas outlined in blue for the MVP. We focused on holiday reminders only; customization of holidays; ability to schedule a card to send in the future; and APN integration for the user-selected holidays. As our first iteration, we would test usage/behavior to inform improvements + our next iteration of feature offerings.

    Design Process

    Target Audience

    New User Activation (i.e., getting new users to send their first card) was our main KPI, so we wanted to gauge performance on new regristrants specifically. We also tracked rate of sending for repeat users.

    Design Goal

    My goal was to create a flexible design that could support not only holiday content, but ultimately future reminder types—birthdays, anniversaries, and custom. Visual assets (that showcase card designs) for each holiday would also be crucial to engagement.

    Assets

    I created all the product-specific assets (right) showcasing card designs for each holiday. Considering the category assets consisted solely of generic representations (left), showing the actual card product was crucial to engaging users—especially for new users who may not know what the product is.


    Performance

    Upon releasing this feature in Q1, send conversion through this feature was 8% greater than through the generic categories on the homepage. We released just in time for Valentines Day, and experienced a 12% increase in V-day card sales through the app compared to the previous year.
