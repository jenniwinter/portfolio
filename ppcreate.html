Paperless Post: Customizing Invitations

About the Product

Paperless Post is an e-commerce product company offering beautifully customizable invitations + online event management tools. As the Product Designer for the desktop “Create” experience, I owned the design tool through which users can build + customize cards to meet their event needs + aesthetic style.



Role

The "Create" feature team consists of 2 PMs, PD (me), QA engineer, and 7 Developers. I was responsible for all UX + visual design for this feature, frequently collaborating with iOS team for feature parity.

Scope

I worked closely with my PMs to develop strategies for increasing value per card (team's priority KPI) for both online + paper cards. We iteratively pushed improvements through gradual rollouts + a/b tests.

Time

I was part of the "Create" feature team for desktop from April 2015-December 2016. Eventually I moved to the iOS team in 2017, where I designed for all features on the mobile platform (including iOS's "Create").

Create Tool Experience

The Users

There are two types of users who enter the Create tool—those looking to design a(n):

  • Online card to be sent to recipient(s) via email.
  • Paper card to be printed in the desired paper stock + quantities, then shipped to a physical address.

    • To account for the difference in use cases for sending online vs. ordering paper, the Create tool offers 2 different user experiences and features for each. The design challenge here is to appeal to the separate needs of online and paper users, while still maintaining a streamlined experience that feels like one Create tool.

    Customizing the Card

    Users may edit card content by using the Text toolset, where text formatting can be adjusted with detailed controls. Users may also select from a set of typefaces and colors that are recommended for the selected card design. Other style variations of the card may be selected through the Card Design toolset.

    Check out the live create tool!


    Premium Add-ons

    We also drove value per card with paid Backdrops designs to set a mood/theme for the background of a card. For added utility, we also offered the ability to include graphics, logos, photos, and additional information on separate cards.


    Customizing the Envelope

    Envelopes offer a personal touch to the often impersonal concept of an e-card. Once added as a premium asset, envelopes can be customized and stylized to fit the occasion. Users can add text on the envelope to address the recipient directly, and select an envelope color, inner liner design, and a digital stamp + postmark for added personalization.


    All the same text, logo, and design options are offered for customizing paper cards as with online, with the exclusion of backdrops + digital stamps/postmarks.


    Considering the Recipient

    Once the user is done customizing, they shift mindsets to their recipient—how will my friends receive + reply to my card? We introduce these delivery-related customizations near the end of the design experience, where users can choose reply functionality needed based on the occasion. These choices inform the next step of the Create experience, where users can:

    Specify event date, time, location

    Setup additional event details

    Preview the recipient view


    Envelope Addressing Feature

    For our 2016 holiday release for the paper Create experience, I designed the Envelope Addressing feature. This feature was in response to a major pain point we identified from extensive market research on our paper users—users with large guest lists were manually writing-out each name + address on envelopes. A beautiful design was not enough—users needed a practical solution to this labor-heavy task.


    Homepage Promotion

    Offering this feature/service was crucial to achieving market parity + baseline growth in 2016. The product output of my designs were featured on the homepage to promote awareness for our target audience. My process + designs are outlined below.


    Design Process

    Design Goals

  • Automate envelope addressing as a user-facing feature.
  • Reduce overhead for internal process.
  • Avoid friction and added time-spent in Create.

    • Target Audience

    We targeted the "Bride" persona. Personas in the wedding category had the largest guest lists, and therefore the greatest need for automated envelope addressing.



    Manual Workflow

    The original labor-intensive workflow done via email communication.

    New Automated Workflow

    Newly designed automated workflow for user-facing feature in Create tool.


    Design

    In designing this feature, we greatly reduced our manual internal process. In order to release by the holiday (5mos design/dev time), we prioritized the address upload feature over offering final previews of the envelope mocks (required extra dev time). The flow begins in the Create tool, where we added just 2 steps (below) to setting up envelope addressing.

    Opt-in to Envelope Addressing

    Select an address formatting style


    Order Page

    Once users finish customizing, they enter the Order page, where a preview of all customized elements is presented for review. We decided to put the address upload functionality in this step in order to separate the customization part (selecting address style in Create) from the functional part (uploading addresses in Order page).

    Error Logic

    Error messaging guides users + eliminates need for manual error check.

    Success Modal

    File reader counts # of addresses so user knows quantity of envelopes to order.


    All Error Types


    Performance

    We started mid-Q2 of 2016, shooting to release beginning of Q4. Due to technical fallbacks, we released a month late in October. However, we exceeded attachment rate projections + supported long-term growth of our paper user base.

    Attachment Rate

  • Projected (for Q4 2016): 16%
  • Actual (for Q4 2016): 19.38%
  • Cumulative (Q4 2016 - Q2 2017): 23.82%

    • Year-over-Year Growth

    Contributed to 51% YOY Growth in gross paper sales over 2016 (compared to 12% in previous year)
